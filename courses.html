Windows Forensics

Windows forensics is a specialized field within digital forensics that focuses on investigating and analyzing digital evidence from Windows operating systems. It involves the systematic collection, preservation, and analysis of data from computers, devices, and networks running Windows to uncover evidence related to cybercrimes, security breaches, unauthorized access, and other digital incidents.

Image Steganography

Image steganography is a technique used to hide secret messages or data within digital images in a way that is not easily detectable by the human eye. It falls under the broader category of steganography, which is the practice of concealing information within other non-secret data to ensure its confidentiality and integrity. Image steganography is particularly popular due to the widespread use of images in digital communication and the internet.

RAM Acquisition using FTK Imager

Acquiring RAM (Random Access Memory) using FTK Imager is a process commonly used in digital forensics to capture the volatile data stored in a computer's RAM. This volatile data can include running processes, open network connections, passwords, encryption keys, and other critical information that is not stored on the hard drive.
